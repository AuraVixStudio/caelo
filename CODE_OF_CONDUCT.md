# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in the Caelo
community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size,
visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity
and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status,
nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and
orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming,
diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our standard

This project adopts the **Contributor Covenant, version 2.1**, as its Code of
Conduct. The full text — including the detailed examples of acceptable and
unacceptable behavior, enforcement responsibilities, scope, and the enforcement
guidelines (Correction / Warning / Temporary Ban / Permanent Ban) — is the
canonical document and is incorporated here by reference:

- https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/

In short: be respectful and constructive, assume good faith, welcome newcomers,
and accept feedback gracefully. Behavior that harasses, demeans, or excludes other
participants is not tolerated, in project spaces and in public spaces when an
individual is representing the project.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be
reported to the project maintainers privately, through the repository's contact
channel (e.g. GitHub's private reporting under the *Security* tab, or a direct
message to a maintainer). All complaints will be reviewed and investigated
promptly and fairly. Maintainers are obligated to respect the privacy and security
of the reporter of any incident.
